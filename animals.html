Map Animals Amenities
Lion

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Panthera leo

The majestic lion is a symbol of strength and courage. These social cats live in prides and are the only cats that display obvious sexual dimorphism.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and savannas
  • Diet: Carnivorous (zebras, wildebeest, antelopes)
  • Lifespan: 10-14 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: A lion's roar can be heard up to 5 miles away
Elephant

Gentle Giant

Scientific Name: Elephas maximus

The Asian elephant is the largest land animal in Asia. They are known for their intelligence, social behavior, and strong familial bonds.

  • Habitat: Forests, grasslands, and swamps
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, fruits, bark)
  • Lifespan: 60-70 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Elephants are known to use tools and have excellent memories.
Gemsbok

Desert Antelope

Scientific Name: Oryx gazella

The gemsbok is a large antelope with long, straight horns and a distinctive facial pattern. They are well adapted to life in arid environments.

  • Habitat: Arid regions and savannas
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, leaves, fruits)
  • Lifespan: 15-20 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Gemsboks can go without water for long periods, obtaining moisture from the plants they eat.
Giraffe

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis

The giraffe is the tallest land animal, known for its long neck and legs. They are social animals that live in groups called towers.

  • Habitat: Savannas and open woodlands
  • Diet: Herbivorous (leaves, fruits, flowers)
  • Lifespan: 25 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: A giraffe's neck is too short to reach the ground, so it has to awkwardly spread its legs to drink.
Warthog

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Phacochoerus africanus

The warthog is a wild member of the pig family found in Africa. They are known for their distinctive tusks and facial whiskers.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and savannas
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, roots, fruits)
  • Lifespan: 5-10 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Warthogs use their tusks to dig for roots and bulbs in the ground.
Gorilla

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Gorilla gorilla

The gorilla is the largest living primate and is known for its strength and intelligence. They are social animals that live in groups led by a dominant male.

  • Habitat: Tropical forests
  • Diet: Herbivorous (leaves, stems, fruit)
  • Lifespan: 35-40 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Gorillas share 98% of their DNA with humans.
Panda

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca

The giant panda is a bear species found in the mountain ranges of China. They are known for their distinctive black and white coloring and their diet primarily consists of bamboo.

  • Habitat: Bamboo forests in mountainous regions
  • Diet: Herbivorous (bamboo, fruits, vegetables)
  • Lifespan: 20 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Pandas have a special wrist bone that functions as an opposable thumb, allowing them to grasp bamboo while eating.
Zebras

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Equus zebra

The zebra is known for its distinctive black and white stripes. They are social animals that live in herds and are native to Africa.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and savannas
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, leaves, shoots)
  • Lifespan: 25 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Zebras are social animals and live in herds for protection against predators.
Koala

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus

The koala is a tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia. They are known for their distinctive appearance and their diet primarily consists of eucalyptus leaves.

  • Habitat: Eucalyptus forests
  • Diet: Herbivorous (eucalyptus leaves)
  • Lifespan: 10-12 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Koalas have a specialized diet and can eat up to 2.5 kg of eucalyptus leaves per day.
Dodo

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Raphus cucullatus

The dodo is an extinct flightless bird that was native to the island of Mauritius. It is often used as a symbol of extinction and human impact on the environment.

  • Habitat: Forests and woodlands
  • Diet: Herbivorous (fruits, seeds, nuts)
  • Lifespan: Unknown (extinct)
  • Fun Fact: The dodo was about 3 feet tall and weighed around 23-39 pounds.
Indian Bustard

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Ardeotis nigriceps

The Indian bustard is a large bird native to the Indian subcontinent. It is known for its impressive size and is often found in grasslands and scrublands.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and scrublands
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, seeds, insects)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: The Indian bustard is one of the heaviest flying birds and can weigh up to 18 kg (40 lbs).

Ostrich

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Struthio camelus

The ostrich is the largest living bird and is native to Africa. It is known for its impressive running speed and inability to fly.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and savannas
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, seeds, fruits)
  • Lifespan: 10-14 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: The ostrich can run at speeds up to 45 mph (72 km/h).
Rhino

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Rhinoceros unicornis

The Indian rhinoceros is a large herbivorous mammal known for its single horn and thick, wrinkled skin. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is primarily found in grasslands and forests.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and forests
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, leaves, fruits)
  • Lifespan: 30-40 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: The Indian rhinoceros is an excellent swimmer and can submerge itself in water to stay cool.
Kangaroo

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Macropus

The kangaroo is a marsupial from the family Macropodidae (meaning "big foot"). They are native to Australia and are known for their powerful hind legs and large tails.

  • Habitat: Grasslands and forests
  • Diet: Herbivorous (grasses, leaves)
  • Lifespan: 6-8 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: Kangaroos use their strong tails for balance while hopping.
Tiger

King of the Jungle

Scientific Name: Panthera tigris

The majestic tiger is a symbol of strength and courage. These solitary cats are known for their striking orange coat with black stripes.

  • Habitat: Tropical forests and grasslands
  • Diet: Carnivorous (deer, wild boar, and other large mammals)
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years in the wild
  • Fun Fact: A tiger's roar can be heard up to 2 miles away
